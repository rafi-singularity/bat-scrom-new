Competition and Anti-Trust

We must not collude with our competitors (directly, indirectly, or via any third party) to fix any elements of pricing, to divide or allocate markets, or influence the outcome of the market. Shakib may have had good intentions, but the Group states information about our competitors can be gathered only through legitimate legal means, and in compliance with competition law.

If Shakib wants to talk to Competitors for any reason, he must talk to the Legal Counsel with proper reasoning before initiating such affiliation or meeting. In this meeting, he must ensure meetings and calls have an agenda, compliance statement, minutes, and attendee list. Even if there is a scenario where competitor is directly or indirectly disclosing information, he should not have access to, he should leave the conversation and report it to the Legal Counsel without using it.
